Papers compress months of work into eight pages of past tense. These write-ups do the opposite: they take each piece of research I've done and rebuild it from the ground up — the problem, the idea, the model, the honest results, and the thing I'd tell you if we were at a whiteboard. Every one is grounded in the real paper and the notebook that produced the numbers.
